**New in v0.3:**
- Sub-group: **Outcome disparities** (with "Disparate impact on protected groups" 1.1 and "Differential performance across populations" 1.2)
- Sub-group: **Representational harm** (with stereotyping 2.1, sentiment disparity 2.2, geographic skew 2.3, **homogenization 2.4 NEW**, harmful content 2.5)
- Sub-group: **Dynamic & Systemic bias** (with feedback loops 3.1)
- Subcategory: **Homogenization of output across groups** (2.4) — new

**Decision on `harmful-content-toxicity`:** Kept as a separate subcategory (2.5) under Representational harm. Conceptually distinct from stereotyping: stereotyping reinforces narratives about specific groups, whereas harmful content/toxicity covers a broader range of outputs (violent, hateful, abusive content) that may not be group-specific. This distinction is preserved in MIT (Subdomain 1.2 "Exposure to toxic content") and NIST 600-1 (#5 "Dangerous, Violent, or Hateful Content").

**Decision on `accessibility-barriers`:** Absorbed into "Disparate impact on protected groups" (1.1). People with disabilities are a protected group, and lack of accessibility is a form of disparate impact on that group. This is consistent with Usman's principle of consolidating related concepts. Accessibility-specific considerations (WCAG, screen reader compatibility, etc.) belong in the operationalisation layer.

## Incident Reporting & Redress

**Status:** DISSOLVED.

| Current concept | v0.3 destination | Action |
|---|---|---|
| absence-of-appeal | Transparency / Explainability / Right to explanation & contestation | → |
| unclear-remediation | Governance / Compliance & process (failure-remediation-gaps) | → |
| ineffective-communication | Transparency / Explainability / Right to explanation & contestation | → |

**Decision on `ineffective-communication`:** Absorbed into the new "Right to explanation & contestation" subcategory (Transparency / Explainability / 1.2). The scope of "ineffective communication with affected parties" overlaps substantively with the right to explanation: ensuring affected individuals are notified, receive an explanation, and have channels to challenge decisions. Folding both into a single concept is cleaner than adding a separate subcategory in Communication & disclosure that would overlap with the new 1.2. The definition of "Right to explanation & contestation" should be written to cover both notification/communication of incidents and the right to contest.

## Open items for implementation

1. **Definitions need updating** to reflect the new structure:
   - Existing concepts that have been moved or renamed (e.g., "Sentiment and quality disparity across groups", "Inadequate disclosure of system capabilities and limitations")
   - Sub-groups that change scope (e.g., Compliance & process expanded with monitoring, remediation, incident response)
2. **New subcategories need definitions:**
   - Homogenization of output across groups
   - Model extraction
   - Data poisoning
   - Right to explanation & contestation
3. **Operationalisation field design:** the YAML field structure for capturing mechanisms (dataset bias, proxy discrimination, etc.) under their parent risk concepts needs to be designed.
4. **Retired status implementation:** the `status: retired` mechanism in the YAML and pipeline needs to be implemented.
5. **External framework mappings:** review and update mappings on moved/renamed/new concepts (e.g., Reliability mappings need to drop the Manipulation references, new "Harmful Misuse" sub-group needs its own mappings, etc.).
